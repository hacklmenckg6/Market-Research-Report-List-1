<p><p>Scanning Tunneling Microscopes (STM) are advanced imaging tools used to observe the surface of conductive materials at the atomic level. They operate based on the quantum tunneling phenomenon, where a sharp metallic tip scans over a surface and measures the tunneling current that occurs between the tip and the surface atoms. This capability allows scientists and researchers to visualize and manipulate materials at nanoscales, making STMs invaluable in fields such as nanotechnology, materials science, and semiconductor research.</p><p>The Scanning Tunneling Microscopes Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.9% during the forecast period. <p><ul><li>Constant-Current Mode</li><li>Constant-Height Mode</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Scanning Tunneling Microscopes (STM) market is primarily segmented into two types: Constant-Current Mode and Constant-Height Mode. In Constant-Current Mode, the microscope maintains a constant tunneling current while adjusting the tip's vertical position to scan the sample surface, ensuring precise topographical images. Conversely, in Constant-Height Mode, the tip remains at a fixed height, measuring current variations to map surface features. Each mode serves distinct applications in material science and nanotechnology, influencing design choices in STM development.</p></p>
