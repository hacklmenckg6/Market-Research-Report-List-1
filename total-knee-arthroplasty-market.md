<p><p>Total Knee Arthroplasty (TKA) involves two main types of implants: fixed-bearing and mobile-bearing. Fixed-bearing implants have a stationary component that provides stability and is commonly used due to their reliability and longevity. They are often recommended for patients with moderate activity levels. In contrast, mobile-bearing implants feature a moving component that allows for greater range of motion and activities, potentially reducing wear and improving outcomes for more active patients. Both types aim to relieve pain and restore function, tailored to individual needs.</p></p>
